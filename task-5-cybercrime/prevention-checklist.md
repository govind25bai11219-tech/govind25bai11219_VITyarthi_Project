Checklist — 10 Actionable Prevention Tips
General Online Safety

 1. Use strong, unique passwords for every account
Use a mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols (e.g., G0v!nd@2026). Never reuse the same password across platforms like Instagram, Google, and college portals. Use a password manager (Bitwarden, Google Password Manager) if needed.
 2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all important accounts
Turn on 2FA for email, social media, and banking apps. This ensures that even if your password is stolen, the attacker cannot log in without your phone's OTP. Go to Settings → Security → Two-Factor Authentication on most platforms.
 3. Never click unknown links sent via WhatsApp, SMS, or email
Phishing links often disguise themselves as college notices, scholarship alerts, or job offers. Before clicking any link, check the URL carefully — scammers use lookalike domains like vit-bhopaI.in (capital I instead of l). When in doubt, open the official website directly in your browser.
 4. Keep your devices and apps updated
Software updates patch security vulnerabilities that hackers actively exploit. Enable auto-updates on your phone and laptop for the operating system, browsers, and apps. Outdated apps are one of the most common entry points for malware.
 5. Be cautious about what you share on social media
Avoid posting your phone number, home address, college ID, or travel plans publicly. Cybercriminals use publicly available personal details to craft convincing scams targeting you specifically (this is called spear phishing).
 6. Use only trusted, secured Wi-Fi networks
Avoid logging into banking apps or college portals on public Wi-Fi at canteens, cafes, or hostels. If you must use public Wi-Fi, connect through a VPN (Virtual Private Network) to encrypt your data. Never conduct sensitive transactions on unsecured networks.
 7. Verify the identity of anyone who contacts you online claiming authority
Scammers often impersonate police officers, bank officials, TRAI (Telecom Regulatory Authority), or university staff. No legitimate authority will demand instant payment, OTP sharing, or personal information over a phone call or WhatsApp message.
 8. Regularly check app permissions on your phone
Many apps request access to your contacts, microphone, camera, and location unnecessarily. Go to Settings → Apps → Permissions and revoke any permissions that seem excessive. A flashlight app does not need access to your contacts.


Financial & UPI Safety (Mandatory Tips)

 9. NEVER share your UPI PIN, OTP, or CVV with anyone — ever
Banks, Google Pay, PhonePe, and Paytm will NEVER ask for your UPI PIN or OTP over a call or message. A common scam in India involves fraudsters posing as customer care agents and asking for OTP to "verify your account." Sharing it gives them full access to your money. Remember: OTP is for sending money — anyone asking for it is trying to steal from you.
 10. Always verify before scanning any QR code for receiving money
Scanning a QR code to RECEIVE money is not how UPI works — QR codes are only used to SEND money. If someone sends you a QR code and asks you to scan it to receive a refund or prize money, it is a scam. You will lose money, not receive it. Always use your UPI ID (VPA) to receive payments.
